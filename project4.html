CCNA Overview

This page highlights my journey through the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) curriculum, focusing on network design, architectures, protocols, and foundational networking knowledge. I have gained practical experience in building local area networks (LANs), implementing security measures, and configuring routers and switches through interactive labs and real-world scenarios.

Introduction to Networks

In the first course of the CCNA series, I learned about network architectures, IP addressing, and Ethernet fundamentals. This foundational knowledge prepared me for associate-level job roles in networking, such as Network Administrator, System Administrator, or Network Engineer.

Understanding the OSI Model

I developed a deep understanding of the OSI model, which is crucial for troubleshooting and configuring network devices. This knowledge is essential for effective communication and operations within networks.

Switching, Routing, and Wireless Essentials

The second course focused on switching and routing, as well as wireless local area networks (WLANs) and security concepts. After completing this course, I became proficient in network operations, configuration, troubleshooting, and mitigating LAN security threats.

CCNA Certification

Completing the CCNA curriculum has equipped me with the skills and knowledge to confidently tackle various networking challenges. I earned my CCNA certification, validating my expertise and opening doors to exciting career opportunities in networking.
